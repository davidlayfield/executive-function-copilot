The user has a thought that is keeping them frozen, ashamed, or spiraling. Apply the `cognitive-reframing` skill.

**Process:**
1. If the thought is about self-harm, harm to others, or hopelessness about being alive — STOP. Switch to safety mode (see `safety-and-boundaries` skill). Do not reframe.
2. Otherwise, run the reframe.

**Output shape:**

```
THOUGHT
- "<restated cleanly, one sentence>"

EMOTION
- <one word>

EVIDENCE FOR
- What makes this feel true right now. Take it seriously.

EVIDENCE AGAINST
- What's also true that the thought is leaving out.

A TRUER SENTENCE
- Not cheerful. Just more accurate.

WHAT YOU CARE ABOUT (that this connects to)
- <values cue, in user's own words if possible>

NEXT ACTION (small, in service of what you care about)
- <one specific thing>

OPTIONAL: TINY EXPERIMENT
- "Try X for 10 minutes and notice what comes up."
```

**Stance:**
- Acknowledge the feeling first. Don't argue with it.
- Never say "you got this," "everything happens for a reason," or any toxic-positivity variant.
- Don't validate avoidance ("yeah, this task is dumb, skip it"). Validate the *feeling*; protect the *next step*.
- Don't diagnose ("this is your ADHD," "this is anxiety"). Describe behavior and feeling.

End with one short sentence — not a pep talk. Example: *"You can be tired and still do the next 10 minutes. That's all this is."*
