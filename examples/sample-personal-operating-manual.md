# Personal Operating Manual — Sample

This is what `/build-operating-manual` produces after a short interview, with a few weeks of weekly-review insights added by the reflection coach.

---

```markdown
# Personal Operating Manual — Alex

## My best work conditions
- Best windows of the day: 9–11am, 3–5pm. Avoid 1–2pm post-lunch crash.
- Best environment: quiet, single monitor, headphones off (music distracts me).
- Tools that actually help: paper notebook for planning, Things for tasks, calendar for blocking.

## My common derailers
- Slack notifications during deep work.
- "Just a quick check" of email at 9am.
- Long meetings (>45m) — I lose the rest of the morning.
- Bad sleep — anything under 6.5 hours.

## Tasks I avoid and why
- Bank / admin / forms: boring, requires pulling up info I don't have memorized.
- Tough conversations: emotionally loaded, I rehearse instead of writing the message.
- Deck design: perfectionism — I keep tweaking instead of finishing.

## What helps me start
- Pre-staging the file the night before.
- Body-doubling — even just "I'm working too" in chat.
- A 10-minute timer.
- Coffee, then immediately the hardest thing.

## What does not help me
- 12-step morning routines. I never finish them.
- New apps.
- Long pep talks. I want the next step, not the speech.
- Color-coded calendars.

## Reminder preferences
- Channels I act on: paper sticky on the keyboard, calendar pop-ups, Things daily list.
- Channels I ignore: email reminders, Slack reminders.
- Cadence: one morning prompt, one mid-day check, one shutdown. More than that = noise.

## Planning preferences
- Plan size that I will actually execute: 1 must, 2 should, 2 could. More than that and I freeze.
- Format I prefer: short markdown, anchor goal at the top.
- Detail before noise: time estimates yes, sub-bullets no.

## Communication preferences
- How I want feedback: direct, specific, no buildup.
- What I do not want: lectures, options paralysis, hustle language, infantilizing tone.

## Energy patterns
- Days/times I have energy: Tue/Wed/Thu mornings. Sunday afternoons for planning.
- Days/times I usually crash: Mon afternoons (recovering from weekend reset), Fri afternoons (overdue for rest).
- Recovery activities that work: 20-minute walk, real lunch away from desk, early bedtime.

## Recovery plans
- After a missed day: tomorrow runs the minimum-viable version of the day. No catch-up sprint.
- After a missed week: redesign — don't push.
- After a hard month: drop the stretch goals, keep the must-dos, talk to my coach.

## Weekly review insights (rolling)
- 2026-03-14: Mornings keep getting eaten by meetings. Three weeks running. Need calendar fix, not willpower.
- 2026-03-07: Deck-as-task = trap. Decks are projects. Break them down.
- 2026-02-28: No-laptop-after-9pm is holding when I pre-stage tomorrow's first task before bed.

## Rules for Claude when coaching me
- Always start with the next 10-minute action. Skip the preamble.
- One must-do. Never default to three.
- Don't moralize about what I avoided. Diagnose and lower the bar.
- If I sound depleted, switch me to recovery mode without negotiation.
- Don't store any health/medical details in this file unless I explicitly ask.
- If I talk about quitting work or hating myself for more than a sentence, pause and check in. Don't coach.
```

---

Notes on what this manual does:

- Sections are short, concrete, written in the user's voice.
- "Rules for Claude" is the most important section — the assistant should re-read it before every planning session.
- Weekly review insights accumulate rolling, oldest can be pruned.
- No medical content. No diagnoses. No medications.
