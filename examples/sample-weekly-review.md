# Sample Weekly Review

This is what `/weekly-review` produces after pulling five daily plans, three shutdowns, and a calendar export.

---

```
WEEK OF March 10–14

Wins (specific)
- Shipped the Karen contract on Tuesday — first time this quarter a deliverable hit on plan day.
- Held the no-laptop-after-9pm rule four nights of five.
- Said no to a Friday meeting that would have eaten the deep-work block.

Dropped balls (without shame)
- Bank follow-up: kept getting bumped by meetings.
- Thursday deck: shipped, but at midnight after a stress sprint.
- Lunch: real food only twice. Bars at desk the other days.

Recurring obstacles
- Mornings get eaten by meetings, then "deep work" lands in the worst window of the day. This is the third week running.

Best focus windows
- Tuesday and Thursday 9–11am, when nothing was scheduled.
- Wednesday afternoon, after the morning calls cleared.

Avoidance themes
- Bank/admin items. Three weeks running. They're not actually hard — they're just boring and require pulling up account info.

Task-size mismatch
- "Finish deck" was on the list four days. It was a project, not a task. Should have been broken into: outline, three slides, polish, send.

Schedule realism (1–5)
- 3. Plans were close to honest, but no buffer for the bank/admin stuff. They keep landing nowhere.

Routines
- Keep: no-laptop-after-9pm. Working.
- Change: morning routine — current version assumes 8am start, reality is closer to 8:45. Move the trigger.
- Remove: weekly "inbox zero" goal. Hasn't held in a month. Replace with "10-minute inbox triage on Mon/Thu."

Top 3 outcomes for next week
- 1. Bank follow-up sent by Tuesday (it's been 3 weeks).
- 2. Thursday's deliverable drafted by Wednesday EOD, not midnight.
- 3. Two real lunches.

Experiments for next week (small, falsifiable)
- "Block 9–10am Tue/Wed/Thu as no-meeting deep work for 5 days. Notice if Thursday's stress sprint repeats."
- "Batch all bank/admin tasks into one 30-minute Friday slot. Notice if they actually get done."
```

Closing note from the assistant: *"You've named the meetings-eat-mornings pattern three weeks in a row. That's not a willpower problem — it's a calendar problem. Want me to draft a polite no-meeting-before-10 policy you can paste into your scheduling preferences?"*

---

Notes on what this review does:

- Wins are specific and counted as data, not cheerleading.
- Dropped balls get one phrase each, not a confession.
- The recurring obstacle is named explicitly with "third week running" — and a structural fix is offered, not "try harder."
- Three outcomes for next week. Not ten.
- Experiments are framed as falsifiable noticing, not commitments.
