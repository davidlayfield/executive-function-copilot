# Sample Daily Plan

This is what `/daily-plan` produces. The user pasted: *"Tuesday. Two meetings, both before noon. Need to send a contract draft to Karen, finish the deck for Thursday, follow up with the bank, and figure out lunch. Energy is medium-low, slept badly."*

---

```
TODAY — Tuesday, March 17

Anchor goal
- Send Karen the contract draft. Everything else is bonus.

Must (1)
- Send contract draft to Karen (~45m)

Should (up to 2)
- Add three slides to the Thursday deck (~30m)
- Reply to bank with current EIN + last bank statement (~10m)

Could (up to 3)
- Make a real lunch (not a bar at the desk) (~20m)
- Clear the Slack mentions tab (~10m)
- Walk for 15 minutes between the two meetings

First 10-minute action
- Open the contract template, paste in Karen's name and the new project scope. Don't write more than that yet.

Time blocks (rough)
- 9:00–9:50: Contract draft for Karen
- 10:00–10:30: Meeting #1
- 10:45–11:00: Bank reply
- 11:15–11:45: Meeting #2
- 12:00–12:45: Lunch + walk
- 1:00–1:30: Three slides for Thursday deck
- 1:30+: Could-do list, in any order

Anti-avoidance notes
- The contract is the most likely thing to slide because it requires a real read-through. Friction-removal: open the template *now*, don't wait for "after coffee." Once it's open you'll start.

Mid-day check-in prompt
- 12:30 — "Is the contract sent? If not, what's actually in the way?"

If I fall behind
- The minimum-viable today: contract sent. That's it. The deck and the bank can wait one day. Lunch still happens.

End-of-day shutdown
- Run /shutdown around 5:00 — close loops, write tomorrow's first task, then stop.
```

Notes on what this plan does:

- One must-do, not five. Medium-low energy + bad sleep means the bar is lower today.
- The 10-minute action is *opening the file*, not "draft the contract." That's the activation-energy move.
- A specific friction-removal note appears for the most-skippable item.
- "If I fall behind" exists *before* falling behind.
- The shutdown is scheduled, not optional.
