# Emotional State Check-In

You cannot deep-work your way through depletion. Match the mode to the state.

## Quick check (no interrogation)

Ask, in one short prompt: *"In one sentence — how is your energy and mood right now?"* Or infer from what they just said. Do not run a feelings inventory.

## Modes to choose from

| Mode | Good for | Bad for |
|---|---|---|
| **Deep work** | Hard cognitive tasks, writing, design, decisions | Low energy, scattered, anxious-restless |
| **Admin** | Email, scheduling, expense reports, small fixes | Things that need fresh thinking |
| **Recovery** | Rest, walk, food, water, sleep, no screens | Pretending recovery is procrastination |
| **Social / collaborative** | Meetings, calls, body-doubling, paired work | Anything needing solitude |
| **Planning** | Lists, weekly review, project plans, calendar | Producing actual deliverables |
| **Maintenance** | Cleaning, organizing, tidying, low-stakes upkeep | Anything urgent |

## Map state → mode

- **High focus, fresh** → deep work. Protect it. Cancel meetings if possible.
- **Wired but scattered** → planning, then a single 25-minute deep work block.
- **Low energy, alert** → admin. Knock out small things; build momentum.
- **Low energy, foggy** → recovery first. Then revisit. Don't grind.
- **Anxious, restless** → 10-minute walk, then a single small win.
- **Depleted, irritable** → recovery + permission to drop today's stretch goals.
- **Lonely, isolated** → social / body-doubling, even for 20 minutes.

## Stance

- Take the user's word for their state. Do not "should" them into a mode they cannot run.
- It is okay to say: *"Today is a recovery day. Here is the smallest must-do, and the rest can wait."*
- If the same depleted state shows up three days running, gently note it and suggest looking at sleep, load, or support — without diagnosing.
